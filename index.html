Migration Arrival and Departure in Australia
(2004 Q1 - 2016 Q4)






The graph illustrates migration trends in Australia from 2004 Q1 to 2016 Q4, showing both population arrivals and departures. Over the years, the number of people arriving in Australia increased steadily, peaking between 2008 and 2012, with over 50,000 people arriving per quarter during that period. After 2012, arrivals show a slight decline, settling around 40,000 to 45,000 per quarter. In contrast, departures remained relatively stable across the entire time frame, ranging between 15,000 and 20,000 per quarter.

Top 5 Migration Destination 2022


  by Percent of Population

The chart displays the top five migration destinations in 2022, showcasing the percentage of each country's population that comprises migrants. The UAE stands out with an impressive 85.3% of its population being migrants, reflecting its significant role as a hub for expatriates, likely due to its economic opportunities and favorable living conditions. Kuwait follows with 69.8%, indicating a similarly high reliance on foreign labor.

China and Saudi Arabia also feature prominently, with 38.1% and 37.3% of their populations, respectively, being migrants. Australia has the lowest percentage at 29.1%, yet it remains a desirable destination for many due to its quality of life and diverse opportunities. This data highlights the varying levels of migration dependency across these nations, with Gulf countries leading the trend significantly.

Countries Migration Origin and Destination 2022

This graph displays migration flows between countries in 2022. The largest origin countries include Mexico (10.80M), India (10.48M), and China (11.45M). The largest destination country is USA, receiving 25.70M migrants, mostly from Mexico and China. Followed by other major destinations UAE (7.10M), Saudi Arabia (7.70M), and Australia (3.41M). Migration from countries like Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Egypt show significant flows to Gulf nations like the UAE and Saudi Arabia.

Overall, the graph highlights global migration trends with key sending countries in Asia and Latin America, and key destinations in North America and the Middle East.

Average Annual Income in USD 2022

As we see on the map, the darker the gradient, the greater the average annual income in USD for that country. Ireland has the highest average annual income with $102,001, the United States of America has $71,055, and Australia with $60,697. We see that although USA and Australia are on the higher end of earnings. Countries like United Arab Emirates (UAE) seem to only earn $44,332 in comparison. So we can attribute some correlation to the average yearly income to the immigration appeal of the given country, but as seen through the UAE, there could be some other determining factors.
